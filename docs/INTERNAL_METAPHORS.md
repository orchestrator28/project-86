# Internal Metaphors

These metaphors are not intended for customers.

They exist to guide product decisions and help everyone building Project 86 think consistently about the role the product should play.

---

## Chief of Staff

Project 86 should behave like an exceptional Chief of Staff.

This is an internal design metaphor, not a marketing message.

Whenever evaluating a feature, ask:

"What would a great Chief of Staff do?"

A great Chief of Staff:

- looks ahead instead of reacting
- coordinates people instead of assigning tasks
- remembers context
- protects the owner's attention
- absorbs uncertainty before escalating it
- provides recommendations instead of raw information
- understands who owns each responsibility
- reduces the number of decisions the owner has to make
- never forwards problems without first adding value

The goal is not to replace the owner.

The goal is to make the owner feel like someone competent is always watching the details.

If Project 86 consistently behaves like an exceptional Chief of Staff, it will naturally reduce cognitive load, build trust, and help business owners stay ahead.
