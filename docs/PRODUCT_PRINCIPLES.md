## Reassure Before You Alert

The first job of Project 86 is to answer:

> "Am I okay?"

If the answer is yes, say so.

If the answer is no, explain why clearly and calmly.

Then explain what happens next.

Project 86 should reduce anxiety, not create it.

It should leave the owner feeling informed, supported, and confident—not overwhelmed.

## Protect the Owner's Attention

Running a business already requires hundreds of decisions.

Project 86 should eliminate unnecessary ones.

Whenever possible, the product should answer:

- Is this important?
- Is this urgent?
- What should I do next?
- Can this wait?

so the owner can focus on decisions only they can make.

## Surface the Hidden Work

A due date is rarely the whole story.

Project 86 should help owners understand what needs to happen before the deadline, including preparation, dependencies, coordination, and estimated effort.

The goal is not to remember the deadline.

The goal is to start at the right time.

## Be the Chief of Staff

Project 86 should behave like an exceptional Chief of Staff.

It looks ahead instead of reacting.
It coordinates instead of merely reminding.
It understands who owns each responsibility.
It remembers how work has been done successfully before.
It reduces the number of decisions the owner has to make.

Above all, it gives the owner confidence that someone is watching the details.

Whenever designing a feature, ask:

"What would a great Chief of Staff do?"

## Don't Forward the Problem

Business owners should never receive raw information when they really need understanding.

Before interrupting the owner, Project 86 should first:

- understand what happened
- determine whether it matters
- identify who usually handles it
- recommend the next step

The owner should never receive:

"Here. Figure it out."

Every interruption should reduce cognitive load, not increase it.

## Never Escalate Without Adding Value

If Project 86 is going to interrupt the owner, it should improve the situation first.

That may mean explaining a notice, identifying the responsible person, finding the right document, estimating urgency, drafting a response, or simply reassuring the owner that everything is okay.

Even when uncertain, Project 86 should perform the first round of thinking before asking the owner to perform theirs.
