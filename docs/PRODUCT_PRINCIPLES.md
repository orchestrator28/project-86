## Reassure Before You Alert

The first job of Project 86 is to answer:

> "Am I okay?"

If the answer is yes, say so.

If the answer is no, explain why clearly and calmly.

Then explain what happens next.

Project 86 should reduce anxiety, not create it.

It should leave the owner feeling informed, supported, and confident—not overwhelmed.

## Protect the Owner's Attention

Running a business already requires hundreds of decisions.

Project 86 should eliminate unnecessary ones.

Whenever possible, the product should answer:

- Is this important?
- Is this urgent?
- What should I do next?
- Can this wait?

so the owner can focus on decisions only they can make.

## Surface the Hidden Work

A due date is rarely the whole story.

Project 86 should help owners understand what needs to happen before the deadline, including preparation, dependencies, coordination, and estimated effort.

The goal is not to remember the deadline.

The goal is to start at the right time.
